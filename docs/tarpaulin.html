AI-Assisted Employment Matching

Scratch Match.

The AI-Assisted Job Matcher for Cebu

A locally-bound, on-premise AI service that connects Cebu job seekers with employers through semantic matching — not keyword gambling. Your data never leaves the machine it runs on.

▲ Province of Cebu, Philippines  ·  UN SDG 8

The Problem

The right people and the right jobs
never find each other.

Cebu's job market is stuck in a spray-and-pray loop. Job seekers blast the same generic CV to every posting, hoping a keyword matches. Employers wade through hundreds of irrelevant applications for a handful of real fits. The result isn't a shortage of jobs or talent — it's a chronic mismatch between what seekers offer and what employers actually need. Persistent unemployment and underemployment aren't accidents. They're symptoms of a broken discovery layer.

How It Works

Three AI primitives. One matching engine.

01
Vectorized Matching
CVs and job descriptions are parsed, split into chunks, and converted into numerical embeddings using nomic-embed-text running locally via Ollama. Qdrant stores these vectors and finds matches by actual semantic meaning — not by hoping the same words appear in both documents.
02
Retrieval-Augmented Advice (RAG)
The built-in AI advisor retrieves relevant chunks from your CV and live Cebu job listings before answering. Every response is grounded in real documents — no hallucinated job titles, no invented companies. Ask it to tailor your CV for a role, or to explain why a job matched you.
03
Local LLM — Mistral 7B via Ollama
All inference runs on the host machine. No cloud API calls, no per-token billing, no subscription. The Mistral 7B model is pulled once and runs entirely offline. This means the system can be deployed in a government data center with zero third-party data exposure.

Built Responsibly

Data privacy by architecture, not policy.

Scratch Match stores all data on the machine it runs on. No data is sent to external APIs. No AI cloud services are used. Seeker contact information is gated — it is only revealed to an employer after they formally record a match. The system is deployable in a government data center and is designed to operate within the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173) framework from day one, with no additional integration work.

Model A
Government Subsidized
Province of Cebu hosts the server. The service is free to all Cebu residents. Costs are covered by local government, similar to a public employment portal. Fits under existing LGU digital services mandates.
Model B
Private — Token System
Each user gets a daily quota of free AI queries. Once the daily limit is reached, additional tokens can be purchased. Tokens cover AI advisor queries and semantic match operations — basic CV upload and job posting remain free.
