The Problem

Cebu's job market is broken in three ways.

Listings exist. Applicants exist. The bridge between them does not, and AI, which could obviously help, has barely been pointed at the problem.

↓
Unemployment
Qualified Cebuano seekers blast generic CVs into the void. Openings sit unfilled for weeks. Both sides give up before the right match ever surfaces, and the city loses productive months it can never get back.

Overqualification
Talent ends up parked in roles far below its ceiling because that was the first opening that called back. Wages stagnate, skills decay from disuse, and ambitious workers eventually leave Cebu entirely to find work that matches what they can actually do.
∅
Underutilized AI
AI is everywhere except where it could matter most. Local hiring still runs on keyword luck and gut feel, while semantic matching, the one job AI is genuinely good at, sits unused. Meaning never enters the loop.

How It Works

From CV to shortlist, in five steps.

No keyword games. Each CV and each posting is read for meaning, embedded into a vector space, and matched on semantic distance, locally, on Cebu hardware.

1
Upload & Parse
Seekers drop in a PDF or DOCX CV. Employers post a job. A local LLM (Ollama) extracts skills, experience, and intent.
2
Embed
Both CV and posting are converted into vector embeddings, a numerical fingerprint of meaning.
3
Match (Qdrant)
A vector database finds the nearest semantic neighbors, the candidates whose meaning, not wording, fits the role.
4
Rank & Explain
An AI advisor scores each match and explains in plain language why it is a fit, useful for both sides.
5
Apply & Iterate
Seekers can ask the advisor to tailor the CV for a specific opening; employers shortlist with a click.

Common Questions
Is this a finished product?
No. It is a prototype built for a CIS 2205 design project. Functional, but actively evolving.
Does my data leave Cebu?
No. All inference, storage, and matching runs on local servers. Nothing is sent to a third-party cloud.
Is it free to try?
Yes. The prototype is open for anyone to test, no sign-up fee, no hidden cost.
What file types are supported?
PDF and DOCX for CVs. Job postings can be pasted as plain text or filled into a form.
Do I need an internet connection?
Only to reach the local server. The matching itself runs fully offline once deployed.
How do I send feedback?
Scan the QR on the back panel, or email the team directly. Every comment helps shape the next iteration.
Privacy by Design
You own your CV. Seekers can delete their profile and embeddings any time. Employers see only what is necessary to match. The whole system is auditable, on-prem, and offline-capable.
